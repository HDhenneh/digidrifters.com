⚠ The United States has the world's most imitated constitutional architecture — separation of powers, an independent judiciary, a free press, and regular elections. But formal structures only partially describe who actually holds power. This atlas maps both the constitutional layer and the parallel architecture of lobbying, corporate power, the permanent national security state, and the informal influence networks that shape policy regardless of who wins elections.
